fear doesn't vanish before you act

By Gilbert Bassey April 5, 2026 3 min read

i wrote A Decent Man in 2018. it took me nearly 8 years to publish it.

not because it wasn't ready. it was ready for most of that time. i just couldn't pull the trigger.

first i told myself i needed a bigger platform. then i told myself i should try traditional publishing. then i got distracted by film, by music, by a hundred other things that felt urgent. but underneath all of it was the same thing: i was afraid of what would happen when i finally put it out.

what if the reception wasn't great? what if i couldn't market it well enough? what if i did everything right and it still didn't land?

so i waited. and waited. and called it patience. called it strategy. called it "not the right time."

now here's the tricky part. some of those reasons were real. i genuinely didn't have the funds to publish properly. editing drained me until AI made it manageable. the platform wasn't there yet. i wasn't lying to myself about everything.

but fear had moved in alongside the real reasons, and they looked so similar that i never checked which was which. the legitimate wait gave fear a place to hide. and it hid there for years.

what finally changed wasn't that the fear left. the real reasons ran out. the funds were there. the tools were there. the audience was growing. and i was still hesitating. that's when i knew it wasn't patience anymore.

so i stopped waiting. not because i felt ready. i just got tired of letting it win.

the novel came out. it made people cry. it sits at 4.37 on Goodreads with over 140 ratings. and the thing i spent years being afraid of turned out to be the thing i was built for.

the masks

fear rarely introduces itself as fear. it shows up wearing other things.

sometimes it looks like perfectionism. you're not hiding, you're polishing. sometimes it looks like overthinking. you're not stalling, you're being thorough. sometimes it looks like wisdom. you're not scared, you're being strategic.

and sometimes it hides behind things that are genuinely true. you really don't have the money yet. you really do need more practice. the timing really isn't right. but when those things change and you still haven't moved, ask yourself what's really stopping you. because at that point, it's probably not patience.

fear doesn't leave first. you don't wake up one morning feeling ready. that morning doesn't come. you act with the fear still there, and it loosens its grip after, not before.

what's one thing you've been "waiting" on that might actually be fear in disguise? i'd love to hear about it in the comments.
