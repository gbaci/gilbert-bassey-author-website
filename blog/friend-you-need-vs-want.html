the friend you need vs the friend you want

By Gilbert Bassey November 27, 2024 4 min read

here's something nobody wants to hear: the friend you want and the friend you need are usually two different people.

the friend you want tells you you're right. they validate your feelings, agree with your perspective, support every decision you make. they're there for you when you need to vent, and they never challenge you. they make you feel good.

the friend you need tells you the truth. even when it's uncomfortable. even when you don't want to hear it. even when it might make you upset with them.

most people will hate you if you tell them the truth about themselves.

and that's the problem. we say we want honest friends, but what we really want are cheerleaders. people who will cosign whatever narrative we're telling ourselves, who will take our side without question, who will never make us feel bad about our choices.

the comfort of agreement

i get it. it feels good to be validated. when you're hurting or confused or making a difficult decision, the last thing you want is someone poking holes in your logic or suggesting you might be part of the problem.

you want someone who will say "you're completely right" and "they're completely wrong" and "you deserve better." you want someone who will make you feel justified in your anger, your hurt, your decisions.

and there's a place for that. sometimes you just need someone to be on your side, no questions asked. sometimes you need comfort more than you need clarity.

but if that's all you ever get, you're not growing. you're just staying comfortable.

the value of honesty

real friends—the ones who actually help you become better—are willing to risk the friendship to tell you what you need to hear. they're the ones who will say "i love you, but i think you're wrong about this" or "have you considered that maybe you're being unfair?" or "i support you, but i don't think this is a good decision."

they don't do this to hurt you. they do it because they care enough to be honest, even when honesty is hard. they'd rather tell you the truth and have you be upset with them than watch you make a mistake and say nothing.

but here's the thing: most people can't handle that. they'll get defensive, feel attacked, accuse their friend of "not being supportive." they'll distance themselves from anyone who doesn't blindly agree with them and surround themselves with people who only tell them what they want to hear.

and then they wonder why they keep making the same mistakes.

choosing wisely

if you're lucky, you'll find friends who can do both. who can hold space for your feelings and also challenge you when you need it. who know when to comfort you and when to call you out. who love you enough to tell you the truth, and who you trust enough to hear it.

but you have to be willing to receive it. you have to be able to sit with the discomfort of being wrong, of being called out, of having your narrative questioned. you have to value growth more than comfort.

because the friend who always agrees with you isn't helping you. they're just making you feel better about staying the same.

be the friend you need

and here's the other side of it: you have to be willing to be that friend for others. to tell them the truth even when it's awkward. to push back when they're being unreasonable. to love them enough to risk them being upset with you.

it's not easy. it's much simpler to just nod along, to validate everything, to avoid conflict. but that's not friendship. that's just being nice. and nice isn't the same as good.

so ask yourself: are you surrounding yourself with people who challenge you, or people who just make you feel comfortable? and are you the kind of friend who tells the truth, or the kind who just tells people what they want to hear?

the answer will tell you a lot about whether you're actually growing or just staying the same.

— Gilbert
