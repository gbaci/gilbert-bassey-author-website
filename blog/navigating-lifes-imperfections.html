navigating life's imperfections

By Gilbert Bassey May 9, 2023 5 min read

here's something nobody wants to accept: life is a losing game. no matter what you choose, you're going to lose something. no matter what path you take, you're giving up all the others. every decision comes with a cost, and there's no way to avoid it.

we spend so much time trying to find the perfect choice, the one that doesn't hurt, the one that gets us everything we want with none of the downsides. but that choice doesn't exist. it never has, and it never will.

the myth of the perfect choice

you can have a high-paying career, but it might cost you time with your family. you can prioritize your relationships, but it might mean sacrificing professional advancement. you can pursue your passion, but you might struggle financially. you can play it safe, but you might regret not taking risks.

every option has a trade-off. every door you walk through means a dozen others close behind you. and we torture ourselves trying to figure out which choice will hurt the least, which path will spare us from regret.

but here's the thing: they all hurt. because choosing means losing. and loss is built into the structure of being alive.

embracing the inevitable

once i understood this, something shifted. i stopped looking for the painless path because i realized there wasn't one. i stopped trying to optimize my life to avoid suffering because suffering is part of the deal.

the only way to win is to accept that i've already lost and to embrace the inevitable pain that comes with living.

this isn't nihilism. it's not giving up. it's the opposite. it's freedom.

because when you stop trying to avoid pain, you stop being paralyzed by the fear of making the wrong choice. you accept that every choice comes with consequences, and you make the choice anyway. you pick the pain you're willing to carry, the trade-offs you're willing to make, the losses you can live with.

every choice is a sacrifice

you want to be a great parent? that means sacrificing time and energy you could have spent on other things. you want to build a business? that means risking stability and comfort. you want deep relationships? that means making yourself vulnerable to loss and heartbreak.

there's no version of life where you get everything and give up nothing. and the sooner you accept that, the sooner you can stop agonizing over which choice is "right" and start asking which trade-offs you're willing to make.

because the question isn't "how do i avoid pain?" the question is "what am i willing to suffer for?"

the losing game is worth playing

just because life is a losing game doesn't mean it's not worth playing. in fact, it's the only game there is. and once you stop trying to win—once you accept that loss is part of the process—you can actually start living.

you can make choices without needing them to be perfect. you can commit to things without needing guarantees. you can love people knowing you might lose them. you can build things knowing they might fall apart. and that's okay.

because the alternative is paralysis. it's standing still, avoiding every risk, refusing to choose because you're terrified of choosing wrong. and that's not safety. that's just a slower kind of loss.

navigating imperfection

so how do you navigate a life built on loss and imperfection? you accept it. you make your choices knowing they're flawed. you commit to things knowing they won't be perfect. you love knowing you'll get hurt. you try knowing you might fail.

you stop waiting for certainty and start moving forward with doubt. you stop looking for the path that doesn't hurt and start choosing the pain you're willing to carry.

because that's the only way through. not around, not over, not under. through.

life is a losing game. but it's also the only game worth playing. and the only way to win is to stop trying to win and start playing anyway.

— Gilbert
