why so serious?

By Gilbert Bassey July 10, 2025 4 min read

i've been thinking about how heavy we make everything. how we turn life into this constant performance review, this endless optimization project where every moment has to matter, every choice has to be the right one, every day has to move us closer to some mythical version of ourselves that we're not sure even exists.

and somewhere along the way, we forget that life is also supposed to be... fun? enjoyable? something other than a grind toward an imaginary finish line?

if you take it too seriously, you might as well be dead already.

that sounds dramatic, i know. but think about it. if you're so focused on where you're going that you can't enjoy where you are, what's the point? you're trading your actual life—the one happening right now—for a future that might never feel as good as you think it will.

the weight we carry

i'm ambitious. i want things. i work hard. but i've also watched that ambition turn into a cage when i let it. when every setback feels like a personal failure. when rest feels like laziness. when i can't enjoy a good day because i'm worried about tomorrow.

there's a version of striving that's life-giving. it's the kind that comes from genuine curiosity, from wanting to grow, from loving the process. but there's another version—the one driven by fear, by proving yourself, by running from the feeling that you're not enough yet—that just sucks the life out of you.

and the worst part? that second kind doesn't even work better. you don't get more done when you're miserable. you don't create better things when you're constantly anxious. you just burn out faster and enjoy it less.

finding balance

i wrote a song called "Feel at Home" about this exact tension. it's about learning to be present, to be okay with where you are even while you're working toward something else. to not postpone your peace until you've achieved enough to "deserve" it.

because here's the thing: there's always going to be another goal. another milestone. another version of success just out of reach. if you can't find a way to be at home in your life as it is, you never will be. not when you get the job, not when you hit the number, not when you finally prove whatever it is you're trying to prove.

this isn't about giving up or settling. it's about recognizing that the point of life isn't just to achieve things. it's also to experience things. to feel things. to actually be here, not just pass through on your way to somewhere else.

lighten up

so maybe the answer is simpler than we think. maybe it's just: lighten up. take the work seriously, but not yourself. care about the outcome, but not so much that you forget to enjoy the process. try hard, but also laugh at yourself when you fail. because you will fail. we all do. and it's honestly not that deep.

life is short and weird and beautiful and hard, and it doesn't owe us anything. the least we can do is not make it harder by treating every moment like a test we might fail.

so take a breath. let yourself off the hook. do the thing you've been putting off because it's "not productive." call the friend. take the walk. write the song. waste an afternoon doing absolutely nothing.

you're allowed to enjoy this. you're allowed to be here, right now, without having earned it.
