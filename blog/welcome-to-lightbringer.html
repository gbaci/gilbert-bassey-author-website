Welcome to Lightbringer

By Gilbert Bassey February 7, 2026 5 min read

I've been thinking a lot about understanding lately.

Not the surface kind—the "I understand" we say to be polite or end an argument. I mean the deeper kind. The understanding that shifts something inside you. That helps you see why people do what they do, including yourself. That brings peace even when circumstances haven't changed.

That's what I want this blog to be: a lightbringer.

Why Lightbringer?

When I wrote A Decent Man, the story was about a man forced to confront the darkness in his past. But the deeper truth was about understanding—understanding why we become who we are, why others hurt us, and whether we can change.

Michael's journey taught me something: understanding doesn't excuse harm, but it does free us from being trapped by it. When we understand ourselves and others deeply, we stop carrying unnecessary weight. We find peace. We become capable of being our best selves.

"The stories that change us aren't the ones that tell us what to think—they're the ones that help us understand what we already feel but couldn't name."

What You'll Find Here

This blog is where I'll explore ideas that help us understand ourselves and others more deeply:

  • Why we do the things we do, even when we know better
  • How to make sense of difficult relationships and situations
  • What it means to be your best self when life gets complicated
  • Finding peace of mind in a world that constantly disrupts it

A Promise

I won't waste your time with surface-level advice or empty positivity. Every essay here will aim to bring genuine understanding—the kind that actually helps you live better, not just feel better temporarily.

I've spent years exploring moral complexity through fiction. Now I want to explore it more directly—to shine light on the questions we all face but rarely discuss honestly.

Let's Talk

The comments section below isn't just decoration. I genuinely want to hear from you. What are you wrestling with? What do you need to understand better? The best insights come when we explore these questions together.

Welcome to Lightbringer. I'm glad you're here.

— Gilbert
